Context

The Problem

TechVentures Co. was managing $12M in annual revenue across 5 sales regions, 4 product lines, and a team of 200+ sales representatives — but had no unified visibility into performance. Data lived in three disconnected CRMs, multiple Excel spreadsheets updated manually each week, and email reports that were often 48 hours out of date.

Sales managers spent 15+ hours per week just gathering and cleaning data. By the time insights reached leadership, markets had already moved. Revenue leakage was happening in plain sight, but no one had the tools to see it.

"We knew we were leaving money on the table — we just couldn't see exactly where. The manual reporting process was our biggest bottleneck to growth." — VP Sales, TechVentures Co.

Goals

Project Objectives

Unified data platform: Consolidate 3 CRM systems and 12 Excel sources into a single, reliable source of truth.
Real-time visibility: Enable live KPI monitoring with <2 hour data refresh for leadership and regional managers.
Self-service analytics: Empower 200+ non-technical sales reps to explore their own performance data without analyst support.
Anomaly detection: Automatically surface underperforming regions, products, and reps before they impact quarterly targets.
Approach

Methodology & Process

1
Discovery & Data Audit (Week 1–2)
Conducted stakeholder interviews with VP Sales, 3 regional managers, and 5 sales reps. Mapped all data sources, identified 47 data quality issues, and defined 22 core KPIs.
2
Data Engineering & Cleaning (Week 3–4)
Built SQL-based ETL pipelines to consolidate CRM data. Resolved duplicates, standardized naming conventions, and created a clean star schema data warehouse optimized for reporting.
3
Dashboard Design & Build (Week 5–6)
Designed and built 6 interconnected Power BI dashboard views using advanced DAX measures. Implemented role-level security so each rep sees only their data, while managers see full regions.
4
Testing, Training & Rollout (Week 7–8)
Conducted UAT with 20 users, incorporated feedback, and delivered training workshops for 3 manager groups. Documented data dictionary and maintenance runbook.

Outcomes

Results & Business Impact

$800K revenue opportunity uncovered in the Southwest region from an undetected pricing discrepancy affecting 340 accounts.
15 hours/week saved per manager — equivalent to nearly 2 full working days returned to strategic selling activities.
18% improvement in forecast accuracy compared to prior quarter using the new predictive trend layer.
200+ users onboarded with 87% weekly active usage rate at 3-month mark — exceptionally high for enterprise BI tools.
Reflection

Challenges & Lessons Learned

The biggest challenge was data quality — the three CRM systems used different account ID formats, making joins unreliable. We solved this with a fuzzy-matching algorithm that correctly linked 94.7% of records automatically, with a human review queue for the remaining 5.3%.

The adoption lesson was profound: technical quality alone doesn't drive adoption. Investing in co-design sessions with actual end-users in week 1 was the single best decision we made. The dashboard we built looks very different from what we'd have designed in isolation — and it's used every single day.
